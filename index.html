Frequently Asked Questions

Get answers to common tax and accounting questions

What documents do I need for tax preparation?

Typically you'll need: W-2 forms, 1099 forms, investment income statements, mortgage interest statements, charitable contribution receipts, business income and expense records, and last year's tax return. We provide a detailed checklist during your consultation.

When should I contact a tax professional?

Contact us if: you're starting a business, experiencing major life changes (marriage, divorce, home purchase), have investment income, own rental properties, are self-employed, or if you've received an IRS notice. Early consultation can save you money and stress.

What's the difference between bookkeeping and accounting?

Bookkeeping involves recording daily financial transactions, while accounting involves interpreting, classifying, analyzing, and summarizing financial data. We provide both services to give you complete financial clarity.

How can you help me save on taxes?

We identify all eligible deductions and credits, implement tax-efficient strategies, help with retirement planning, advise on business structure optimization, and provide year-round tax planning to minimize your tax liability legally and ethically.
